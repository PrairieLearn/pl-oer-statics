When improperly designed, a concrete dam idealized as a concrete block on top of a flat surface may overturn from the inner water pressure built up. Hence, a safy factor (SF), the stabilizing moment due to the weight of the dam divided by the overturning moment by the water pressure force about the tipping point of the dam, $A$, is used when designing the dam to prevent such event from happening.
